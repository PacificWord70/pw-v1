Summer Engineering Project Program
Software Engineering Intern

Over the summer of 2018, I participated as one of 25 interns in Rockwell Collin's Summer Engineering Projects Program (SEPP). I used C++ and Python to modify company flight management software for a semi-autonomous fixed wing drone. I worked with three teammates to explore the possibility of transforming the existing software into a civilian commercial product. My team and I worked with two other teams to integrate each of our experimental projects onto one physical system. Our team used Agile to delegate work to meet summer deadlines. And at the end of the summer, we displayed our work to company executives through a final presentation and written report.



Undergraduate Teaching Assistant

I was a TA for CS180 and CS180x. CS180 is an the introductory computer science course for freshman. CS 180x is the online version of CS180 for high schoolers and other students around the world. These classes uses object-oriented Java to teach programming basics and principles. Some topics include classes, methods, inheritence, threads, Android development, and networking. Mentoring Aspiring Girls in Computing

MAGIC is a service learning class in which I teach local high school girls about computer science. I work with students to teach them about different coding languages, techniques, and opportunities. Mentoring these high school girls allows me to inspire other women to go into the tech field. MAGIC is working to create a closer community to give these students the confidence and knowledge to pursuit their interests.


Women in High Performance Computing
Student Cluster Competition 2018

I attended the Supercomputing 2018 conference in Dallas, Texas, as a Student Cluster Competition Participant. The Student Cluster Competition(SCC) is a competition for undergraduate students to build and run a cluster. Here is a link to the official overview. I had a truly amazing experience learning about high-performance computing and machine learning. My team and I really enjoyed being the first all woman team from Purdue University and bringing our own flare to the event. The applications we ran during the competition were: HPL, HPCG, Horovod, OpenMC, WRF, and Seisol.
